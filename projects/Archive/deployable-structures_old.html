Deployable Structures Research

Novel connection design for disaster relief structures

Project Type

Research & Development

Institution

Cal Poly San Luis Obispo

Year

2020-2022

Role

Graduate Research Assistant

Research Overview

This graduate research project focused on developing innovative connection systems for rapidly deployable emergency shelter structures. The research addressed critical needs in disaster relief scenarios where traditional construction methods are impractical due to time constraints, resource limitations, and challenging site conditions.

Research Objectives

  • Design lightweight, portable connection systems for rapid assembly
  • Develop standardized components that require minimal tools and expertise
  • Ensure structural integrity under various loading conditions including wind and seismic forces
  • Create cost-effective solutions suitable for mass production and distribution

Methodology

  • Finite Element Analysis (FEA) using ABAQUS for connection behavior modeling
  • Physical testing of prototype connections under various load scenarios
  • Iterative design process based on testing results and performance optimization
  • Collaboration with disaster relief organizations for practical requirements assessment

Key Innovations

  • Tool-free assembly system using innovative locking mechanisms
  • Modular design allowing for various structure configurations
  • Weather-resistant materials and coatings for long-term durability
  • Compact packaging design for efficient transportation and storage

Impact & Recognition

  • Baker-Koob Award Recipient (2022) for outstanding graduate research
  • Potential for patent application on novel connection design
  • Research findings contributed to ongoing disaster relief infrastructure development
