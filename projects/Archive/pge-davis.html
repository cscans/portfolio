PGE Davis

Utility infrastructure structural design project

Project Type

Utility Infrastructure

Location

Davis, California

Year

2024

Role

Structural Engineer

Project Overview

The PGE Davis project involved structural engineering services for Pacific Gas & Electric utility infrastructure improvements in Davis, California. This project required specialized knowledge of utility construction standards, high-voltage equipment support structures, and coordination with electrical engineering requirements.

Project Scope

  • Structural design for electrical substation equipment foundations
  • Support structure design for high-voltage transmission lines
  • Underground utility vault structural design
  • Coordination with PGE engineering standards and specifications

Design Requirements

  • Compliance with PGE construction standards and specifications
  • High-voltage electrical clearance requirements
  • Seismic design for critical utility infrastructure
  • Environmental considerations for outdoor electrical equipment

Structural Solutions

  • Reinforced concrete foundations designed for equipment vibration and seismic loads
  • Steel support structures with corrosion-resistant coatings
  • Precast concrete utility vaults with watertight construction
  • Grounding system integration with structural elements

Key Challenges

  • Working within live electrical facility constraints
  • Meeting stringent utility industry safety standards
  • Coordinating with multiple utility system upgrades
  • Ensuring minimal service disruption during construction
